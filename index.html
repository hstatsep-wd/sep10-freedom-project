Preservation of Information


What is Information?

Information is what makes up most of human history, which can come in many different forms, from the text messages on your phone to programs on computers, to even non technological things like books, music and art. the problem is that most data will degrade in a certain amount of time, which means it would be lost forever. the goal of this page is to share ideas of new ways of storing data for future generations and also showing the history of data storing technology.

History of Digital Information

1820: Punch Cards

  • Original use was meant for the making of clothing
  • Was a major aid the 1890 election in the united states
  • Was the original way to program computers

1887: Vinyl Record

  • Used vibrations of a record to play music
  • Still used today with a popularity amongst music enthusiasts
  • Can store up to 400MB of data at max

1951: Magnetic Tape

  • Is way faster than punch cards
  • Was developed when computers became too fast to use punch cards
  • Can store up to 80Kb of storage (about the size of a jpeg photo)

1967: Floppy Disk

  • Used similar technology to a magnetic tape
  • Had a storage capacity of 240 kb while being more compact
  • Uses a similar technology would be used in cds

1969: The Internet

  • Was made as a way for the government to communicate with other branches during the cold war
  • Was only used early on by academic organizations who had the trust from the government
  • The true public internet was born in 1983

1977: Voyager 1 record

  • Was made to last billions of years
  • Has information about earth on it and photos
  • Teaches you how to decode it, and the location of earth and our solar system

1982: Compact Disc

  • Was thought to not sell well at the time
  • Had better quality audio files than any other standard that the time
  • Was able to store music, movies, software, and photos

1991: World Wide Web

  • Allowed people to access information quickly within their own home
  • Was actually made in 1983 but became public in 1991
  • The first website is still up today

2013: 5D optical storage

  • Can last 10^20 years
  • Can handle temperatures up to 1000 degrees celsius
  • Can store up to 360 terabytes of data
